TV Energy Consumption Insights

This page presents a story based on an analysis of televisions available in the Australian market. The aim is to help environmentally-conscious consumers understand which models are more energy-efficient and what factors influence energy consumption.

Our Audience

This information is tailored for Australian consumers who are concerned about their energy usage and environmental impact. These users are likely to prioritize long-term cost savings and want to reduce their electricity bills by choosing efficient televisions.

What They Want to Know

  • Which screen technologies (LED, OLED, etc.) are most common and efficient?
  • What are the most common screen sizes?
  • Which brands offer the widest variety of models?
  • How does screen size relate to energy usage?
  • Are star ratings a good indicator of efficiency?

Key Findings

  • Screen Technology: LED screens are the most frequent and generally consume the least power.
  • Screen Sizes: The most common sizes are between 40" and 65", with 55" being the most popular.
  • Brand Variety: Samsung, LG, and Sony offer the largest number of unique models.
  • Power vs Size: Larger screens typically use more power, though modern technologies reduce the gap.
  • Star Rating vs Size: Star ratings decrease slightly as screen size increases, but efficient large TVs do exist.

Conclusion

Consumers can make smarter energy choices by selecting LED TVs, considering mid-range screen sizes, and reviewing star ratings. Brand choice also plays a role, with some manufacturers placing greater emphasis on energy efficiency.
